Automata Theory

Automata theory studies abstract machines (automata) and the problems they can solve. It formalizes computation using simple mathematical models such as finite automata, pushdown automata, and Turing machines, and explores languages, decidability, and complexity.

In short: Automata are simple models that help us understand what problems computers can and cannot solve.
